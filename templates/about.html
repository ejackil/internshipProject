Our Story

The Beginning

John Finch and Steve Goose, childhood friends, always dreamed of owning the best restaurant ever. As kids, they had crazy, creative ideas, like goose-themed restaurants, which seemed far-fetched. Four years ago, on a winter's evening, they decided to make this dream a reality.

December, 2020

The Dream Continues

Just over a year after reconnecting, John and Steve nearly lost hope of finding a suitable location. Then, on a morning stroll, they discovered a rundown boat house by a stunning lakeside. They immediately knew this was the perfect spot for Finch And Goose.

January, 2022

The Game Plan

After countless hours of hard work, the finish line was finally within reach. They poured their hearts into renovating the boathouse, preserving its charm while adding modern touches. With a unique menu, a stunning website, and top chefs from around the world, they were ready to take over.

October, 2022

The Current Status

As of now, business is thriving! Our menu attracts customers from around the world, and our restored rustic site sets the perfect backdrop for all. With events through the year and futuristic elements like robotic waiters, our restaurant's success continues.

July, 2024

Self-Cleaning Surfaces

Enhancing Hygiene and Sustainability with Cutting-Edge Nano-Technology

Self-cleaning surfaces in our restaurant, enabled by cutting-edge nano-technology, represent a significant advancement in maintaining cleanliness and hygiene. These surfaces repel stains and sanitize tables, countertops, and floors between uses. ... Sensors embedded within these surfaces detect spills and trigger automated cleaning processes, efficiently managing cleanliness without the need for harsh chemicals. This not only enhances the hygiene of our establishment but also aligns with our commitment to environmental sustainability. The automated cleaning mechanisms conserve water and materials, making the cleaning process both eco-friendly and efficient.

Moreover, this self-cleaning technology contributes to long-term cost savings by reducing the frequency of manual cleaning and the need for chemical cleaning agents. This ensures that our restaurant remains spotless and sanitary at all times, providing a consistently pristine dining experience for our patrons. The reduction in the use of harsh chemicals also means a healthier environment for both our staff and customers, minimizing exposure to potentially harmful substances.


Holographic Menus

Dining with Interactive 3D Visuals and Real-Time Translation

Implementing holographic menus here, at Finch And Goose, enhances the dining experience by offering interactive, 3D visuals of dishes, allowing guests to explore ingredients, preparation methods, and dietary information in an immersive and engaging manner. ... One of the standout features of holographic menus is their ability to offer real-time translation for international guests, breaking down language barriers and ensuring that all patrons can fully understand the menu offerings. Additionally, these menus display comprehensive nutritional facts, allergen warnings, and sourcing details, helping guests make informed choices that align with their dietary needs and preferences. This innovative approach not only enhances the dining experience but also contributes to sustainability. By eliminating the need for printed menus, it significantly reduces printing costs and minimizes paper waste, supporting our commitment to environmentally friendly practices. The digital nature of holographic menus also allows for easy updates and modifications, ensuring that the menu always reflects the latest offerings without the need for reprinting. Furthermore, holographic menus provide additional ambient content during wait times, keeping guests entertained and engaged. This can include chef’s specials, upcoming events, or even behind-the-scenes videos of the kitchen, adding an extra layer of interest and excitement to the dining experience.
